<p><p>Scanning Probe Microscopy (SPM) encompasses a range of techniques used to visualize surfaces at the atomic and molecular levels. Key market types include Atomic Force Microscopes (AFM), which measure surface topography by monitoring forces between a probe and the sample; Scanning Tunneling Microscopes (STM), which utilize tunneling current to image conductive surfaces; and Near-field Scanning Optical Microscopes (NSOM), which combine optical and scanning techniques to achieve high-resolution imaging beyond the diffraction limit. These technologies are essential in materials science, biology, and nanotechnology.</p></p>
