<p><p>Sintered high flux tubes for heat exchange are categorized into two primary types based on their surface composition: "Sintered Inner Surface" and "Sintered Outer Surface." Sintered inner surface tubes have a porous layer on the inside, enhancing heat transfer efficiency by increasing the surface area for fluid interaction. Conversely, sintered outer surface tubes feature a porous exterior, improving heat dissipation to the surrounding environment. Both types optimize thermal performance in various applications, including industrial and HVAC systems.</p></p>
