<p><p>The non-absorbable suture material market is categorized into two main types: synthetic and natural materials. Synthetic materials, such as nylon, polyester, and polypropylene, are widely preferred for their strength, minimal tissue reactivity, and long-lasting properties. Natural materials, like silk and cotton, offer biocompatibility but can provoke more tissue response and are generally less commonly used in modern surgical practices. Both categories serve specific surgical needs, with synthetic materials dominating due to their advantages in performance and reliability.</p></p>
