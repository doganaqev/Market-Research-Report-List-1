<p><p>The anti-infective agents market consists of three main types: antibacterial, antifungal, and antiviral drugs. Antibacterial drugs target bacterial infections, combating diseases like pneumonia and tuberculosis through mechanisms that inhibit growth or kill bacteria. Antifungal drugs address infections caused by fungi, such as athlete's foot or candidiasis, often disrupting fungal cell walls or metabolism. Antiviral drugs prevent or treat viral infections like influenza and HIV by inhibiting viral replication or entry into host cells, thus supporting the immune response.</p></p>
